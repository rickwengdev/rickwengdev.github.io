<p>People often ask me:</p>
<blockquote><p>"Why get a U.S. credit card?"</p></blockquote>
<p>My usual answer: because I wanted to build a second financial identity.</p>
<p>But later I realized the real answer is not America.</p>
<p>It is:</p>
<p><strong>I do not want my life to depend on a single financial system.</strong></p>

<hr />

<h3>It Started with a Frozen Account</h3>
<p>In high school, I received funds from a problematic source and nearly all my Taiwan bank accounts became unusable.</p>
<p>What worried me most was not the money.</p>
<p>It was:</p>
<blockquote><p>If I need access to funds in the future, do I have any other options?</p></blockquote>
<p>That was when I first understood:</p>
<p>Any system with only one entry point can become a <strong>Single Point of Failure</strong>.</p>

<hr />

<h3>I Started Building a Second Financial System</h3>
<p>Many people get U.S. credit cards for:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Rewards</li>
  <li>Miles</li>
  <li>Sign-up bonuses</li>
</ul>
<p>That was not my focus.</p>
<p>What I cared about was:</p>
<ul>
  <li>U.S. credit history</li>
  <li>U.S. bank accounts</li>
  <li>U.S. brokerages</li>
  <li>U.S. payment capability</li>
</ul>
<p>Together, they represent one thing:</p>
<p>I began to have a second financial infrastructure that can operate independently.</p>

<hr />

<h3>Later, Beyond the U.S.</h3>
<p>After building a U.S. financial identity, I did not stop.</p>
<p>I started building in:</p>
<ul>
  <li>United States (US)</li>
  <li>Hong Kong (HK)</li>
  <li>United Kingdom (UK)</li>
</ul>
<p>Not because I live there.</p>
<p>Not because I plan to immigrate.</p>
<p>But because:</p>
<blockquote><p>Every mature financial market I add is one more option.</p></blockquote>
<p>I am not collecting bank accounts.</p>
<p>I am reducing my dependence on any single financial system.</p>

<hr />

<h3>But I Am Not Building a Backup</h3>
<p>Many people assume I am building a backup.</p>
<p>I am not.</p>
<p>I do not use all financial systems at once in daily life.</p>
<p>What matters is:</p>
<blockquote><p>I know that if I ever need to, I can start over.</p></blockquote>
<p>What I pursue is not redundancy.</p>
<p>It is <strong>Recoverability</strong>.</p>

<hr />

<h3>Internationalization Is Not About Travel</h3>
<p>Many people think internationalization means:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Using cards abroad</li>
  <li>Travel</li>
  <li>Working overseas</li>
</ul>
<p>I prefer a different definition.</p>
<p>Internationalization means your financial capability no longer exists in only one country.</p>
<p>Your income can be cross-border.</p>
<p>Your investments can be cross-border.</p>
<p>Your assets can be cross-border.</p>
<p>Then your financial identity should be cross-border too.</p>

<hr />

<h3>I Still Trust Banks</h3>
<p>People ask: "So you do not trust banks?"</p>
<p>No.</p>
<p>I still use banks and still trust they work most of the time.</p>
<p>I just do not believe any single system will never fail.</p>
<p>Banks change policies.</p>
<p>Companies collapse.</p>
<p>Platforms shut down.</p>
<p>Even countries can change the rules.</p>
<p>The point is not predicting which one will break.</p>
<p>It is:</p>
<blockquote><p>When one fails, do you still have other options?</p></blockquote>

<hr />

<p>A credit card is not the goal.</p>
<p>A bank is not the goal.</p>
<p>What I am building is a person who <strong>does not depend on a single financial system</strong>.</p>
